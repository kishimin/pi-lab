## 2. Project Concept

### 2.1 Background

- [Why are we building this project?]
  - To create slightly strange and amusing web experiences centered around π
  - To provide an experience where users can play with mathematics rather than study it

- [What inspired this project?]
  - π has many characteristics that can be turned into playful content, such as its non-terminating decimal representation and various approximations
  - We believed that combining π with everyday concepts could create meaningless yet entertaining web experiences

### 2.2 Experience We Want to Provide

- [What kind of experience do we want to provide?]
  - A slightly absurd experience built around π
  - An experience that can be understood within seconds and enjoyed casually
  - An experience that makes users smile
  - An experience users want to share with or show to someone else

- [Why do we want to provide this experience?]
  - To let users enjoy interactive experiences unique to the web
  - To make π feel like a familiar and playful subject, even for people without a strong background in mathematics
  - To create small moments of humor and spark conversations in everyday life

### 2.3 Target Users

- [Who do we want to use this project?]
  - People who enjoy math-related jokes and trivia
  - People who enjoy pointless web services
  - People who enjoy unusual web experiences
  - People looking for fun content to share on social media

- [When would they use it?]
  - When they discover PiLoop on social media
  - When they have a few spare moments and want something quick to play with
  - When they want to send something amusing to a friend or partner
  - When they are looking for a conversation starter

### 2.4 Value We Want to Provide

- [What do we want to achieve with this project?]
  - Provide short, memorable web experiences centered around π
  - Create playful experiences by combining mathematics with everyday life

- [What value does it provide to users?]
  - Users can enjoy the experience without mathematical knowledge
  - Users can play for a short time and have a small laugh
  - Users can share the same experience with others through a URL

- [How is it different from existing web content?]
  - Instead of explaining π, the project uses π itself as a source of play
  - It combines mathematics, communication, and everyday concepts
  - It prioritizes experiences that are "not useful, but worth sharing"
